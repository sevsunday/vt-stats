BulletHit.distance_to_target — brainstorming doc
================================================

Source field
------------
`BulletHit.distance_to_target` (float, world units) was added in the v2
statsgate schema (alongside the unified DamageDealt + Race enum +
shutdown_requested fields). It records the line-of-fire distance from
shooter to victim at the instant the bullet/projectile struck.

Today it is captured-only:
- Pipeline aggregates a per-match summary at `match.bullet_hit_distance`:
    { count, with_distance, mean, max }
  on every match -- v1 sessions report `with_distance=0, mean=null,
  max=null` because the field is absent from the legacy proto. v2
  sessions populate all four.
- No UI consumer reads the field. No VTSR-T axis depends on it. No
  highlight card uses it.

This file enumerates concrete future uses worth prototyping when the
v2 corpus is big enough to do statistically-meaningful analysis. The
availability gate for any consumer is the same shape as
`has_target_lock_data`: pipeline emits a top-level boolean
`has_bullet_hit_distance` or per-match `bullet_hit_distance.with_distance
> 0` and consumers check before drawing.


Section 1 — VTSR-T axis ideas
-----------------------------

The current Thug composite has 8 axes. Distance opens up at least two
genuinely orthogonal signals:

1.1  Distance-weighted accuracy (replacement for or sibling to
     `thug_accuracy`). Weights each hit by `min(distance / 100, 3)` or
     a similar bucketed curve. A 200m sniper shot counts more than a
     5m point-blank. Neutralizes the "spray at touching range" pattern
     that elevates the current accuracy axis. Implementation: in the
     bullet_hit loop, accumulate `weighted_hits[s64][weapon] += w(dist)`
     alongside the existing `player_pvp_shots_hit[s64][weapon] += 1`.

1.2  Engagement-range diversity. Standard deviation of a player's
     hit-distance histogram (with weapon class filtering, so spread
     across weapon archetypes doesn't dilute the signal). Captures the
     "swiss army knife" thug who plays at multiple ranges effectively
     versus the specialist who only ever fights at one range. Cap at
     N hits to avoid noise floor.

Both depend on having a *weapon-normalized* baseline -- a Hornet hits
at long range by nature; an MG hits at short range. The same v2.3
`thug_accuracy` weapon-normalization step is the precedent: compute the
lobby-wide per-weapon median distance, z-score each player's mean
against it.


Section 2 — Per-match stat ideas
--------------------------------

2.1  Per-weapon engagement range stats in `weapon_meta`. For each
     weapon ODF, emit `mean_distance`, `p50_distance`, `p95_distance`.
     Surfaces the actual playstyle deployment of each weapon (rocket
     primarily at >150m, MG primarily at <30m) versus what the ODF
     specs say.

2.2  Per-player engagement-range histogram in `personal.*`. Buckets
     [0..50m, 50..150m, 150..400m, >400m] with hit counts +
     pvp_hits. Drives a small bar in the Player Leaderboard cell,
     showing each player's range distribution.

2.3  "Longest sniper hit" milestone in the kill_feed. When a kill
     follows a BulletHit whose `distance_to_target > 400m`, append a
     `long_range_kill: true` flag to the kill_feed entry. Renders as
     a chip in the feed UI.


Section 3 — Highlight card refinements
--------------------------------------

3.1  "Sharpshooter" (currently fires on weighted PvP accuracy >= 0.40
     w/ shot count gates) gains a distance-floor variant: an
     additional `the_sniper` highlight that requires
     `mean_distance(pvp_hits) > 150m AND pvp_accuracy > 0.30`. Same
     scoring model, different rendering ("nailed shots at range").

3.2  "Chris Kyle" (currently snipes-based) can degrade to firing on
     the sniped-victim distance OR a long-range BulletHit on a
     pilot ODF. Today many matches don't have snipes; this opens a
     fallback signal so the card is more often eligible.

3.3  Per-weapon "best of weapon X" cards (e.g. "best Hornet pilot")
     gain a tiebreaker: same hit-rate ⇒ longer mean distance wins.
     Surfaces skill on long-range weapons where short-range opportunism
     isn't the differentiator.


Section 4 — Data-quality caveats
---------------------------------

- Distance is the LINEAR shooter→victim distance at impact. Curve-line
  weapons (mortars, lobbers) get the straight-line distance, not the
  flight-path arc.
- Self-damage / friendly-fire BulletHits still emit distance values;
  any analytics should filter on `is_pvp_hit AND shooter_team !=
  victim_team`.
- v1 sessions never set the field; all analytics need a per-match
  availability gate. The simplest gate is the pipeline's
  `match.bullet_hit_distance.with_distance > 0`; the dashboard's
  cross-match aggregator can mirror this onto the aggregate
  `meta.has_bullet_hit_distance: bool` so the All Matches view's
  range visualisations gracefully omit when no v2 matches are in the
  filter set.
- For map-scale normalization, divide by the match's
  `base_to_base_distance` (in `match.base_to_base_distance` from the
  positioning block). A 200m shot on Octavia is "across the map"; a
  200m shot on Bunker Trip is barely past base.


Section 5 — Implementation order (suggested)
--------------------------------------------

When v2 corpus reaches ~50 matches:
  1. Plumb `match.bullet_hit_distance` into the All Matches aggregator
     as a passthrough; add `meta.has_bullet_hit_distance` boolean.
  2. Add `personal.distance_buckets` to the per-match leaderboard
     (Section 2.2). Cheap; renders well as a sparkline.
  3. Add weapon-meta distance stats (Section 2.1). Drives the docs
     page's weapon table.

When v2 corpus reaches ~150 matches:
  4. Prototype the VTSR-T distance-weighted accuracy axis as a
     sandbox sibling to `thug_accuracy` (Section 1.1). Compare both
     side-by-side in a hidden card before deciding to replace.
  5. Add `the_sniper` highlight card (Section 3.1).

When v2 corpus saturates:
  6. Decide whether engagement-range diversity (1.2) earns its own
     axis or feeds into thug_accuracy as a third weight term.
